Case Study

Meet Zestify

Zestify — Immunity Boost Box
Australian Premium Cold-Pressed Juice  ·  E-Commerce  ·  $79/month subscription

A Brisbane-based health startup preparing to launch its flagship product: a curated monthly box of cold-pressed juices made from certified organic Australian produce. They have a working Shopify store and a $5,000 launch budget — but zero brand content, no social presence, and no campaign assets.

Target Audience

Health-conscious urban Australians, 25–40 years old, earning $70K+, active on Instagram and TikTok. Motivated by wellness and sustainability.

Campaign Objective

Generate 500 first-time subscriptions within 30 days of launch. Drive traffic via paid social and organic content. All assets must be AI-generated.

KAPLAN
3
Why This Matters

The Business Problem

Every campaign exercise should start with a clearly defined problem. Generative AI is not a creative toy — it is a production tool. The question is always: what problem does this content solve?

Problem 1 — Awareness

Nobody knows Zestify exists. There are no ads, no posts, no search presence. Organic reach alone cannot achieve 500 subscriptions in 30 days.

Problem 2 — Content

Paid social requires creative assets — images, video, copy. Hiring a production agency costs $10,000–$30,000. The $5,000 budget cannot cover that.

Problem 3 — Speed

The product is ready to ship today. Every week without a campaign is lost revenue. Traditional production takes 4–8 weeks. AI production takes hours.

Your role: You are a small AI-native marketing agency. You will solve all three problems — today — using ChatGPT, Suno, and an AI video tool. Your output must be ready to publish.

Task 1A — ChatGPT (chatgpt.com)

Writing the Campaign Copy

Starter Prompt — Ad Headline

Write a punchy 6-word headline for an Australian cold-pressed juice subscription called "Immunity Boost Box" by Zestify. The audience is health-conscious millennials. Make it feel fresh and energetic.

Starter Prompt — Instagram Caption

Write a 3-line Instagram caption for Zestify's Immunity Boost Box. Include a call to action to subscribe. Tone: warm, authentic, health-focused. Add 5 relevant hashtags at the end.

After generating

  • Try changing the tone: "make it more urgent" or "make it more playful"
  • Ask for 3 alternative headlines and pick the strongest
  • Ask ChatGPT: "What makes this headline effective?"
  • Save your best copy — you will need it for the pitch
Remember: These prompts are intentionally simple. You will refine them in Week 7 using the CLEAR framework. The gap between today's output and Week 7's output will be visible and measurable.

Quantitative Challenge

Can We Hit 500 Subscriptions with $5,000?

Work through this with your group. Use the KPI benchmarks from the previous slide.

The scenario

  • Total ad budget: $5,000 AUD
  • Average CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions): $15
  • Click-through rate (CTR): 2%
  • Conversion rate (CVR): 3%
Calculate:
Total impressions = $5,000 ÷ $15 × 1,000
Total clicks = impressions × 2%
Total subscriptions = clicks × 3%

Do you hit 500?

What changes the result?

  • A stronger image reduces your CPM (better creative gets cheaper distribution from Meta's algorithm)
  • A more specific headline increases CTR
  • A better landing page increases CVR — but that is outside your campaign assets
  • Music that drives shares increases organic reach at zero cost
Discussion: Which of your four assets, if improved, would have the biggest effect on reaching 500 subscriptions?
