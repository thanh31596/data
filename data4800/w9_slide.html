How Naive Bayes Decides

It looks at each word and asks: "Does this word make positive or negative more likely?"

Example Review: "The food was amazing but service slow"

amazing ‚Üí 80% positive
food ‚Üí 60% positive
slow ‚Üí 20% positive
Combine all probabilities ‚Üí Final prediction

The algorithm weighs all the evidence to make a decision!

The Math Behind It (Don't Worry!)

Here's the formula, but think of it as a recipe for combining evidence

P(Category | Words) ‚àù P(Category) √ó P(Word‚ÇÅ | Category) √ó P(Word‚ÇÇ | Category) √ó ...

In plain English:

P(Category) = How common is this category overall?

P(Word | Category) = How often does this word appear in this category?

Multiply everything = Combined evidence

Key insight: We multiply probabilities because we assume words are independent (the "naive" part!)

Question: What's the probability of each class when neither word appears (Word1=0, Word2=0)?

Step-by-Step Naive Bayes Calculation

Calculate P(Class | Word1=0, Word2=0)

Step 1: Count class frequencies from training data
‚Ä¢ Positive: 4 out of 10 ‚Üí P(Positive) = 0.4
‚Ä¢ Negative: 3 out of 10 ‚Üí P(Negative) = 0.3
‚Ä¢ Neutral: 3 out of 10 ‚Üí P(Neutral) = 0.3
Step 2: Calculate P(Word1=0 | each class)
‚Ä¢ P(Word1=0 | Positive) = 1/4 = 0.25 (opinions 4 out of 1,2,3,4)
‚Ä¢ P(Word1=0 | Negative) = 3/3 = 1.0 (all negative have Word1=0)
‚Ä¢ P(Word1=0 | Neutral) = 2/3 = 0.67 (opinions 8,9 out of 8,9,10)
Step 3: Calculate P(Word2=0 | each class)
‚Ä¢ P(Word2=0 | Positive) = 2/4 = 0.5 (opinions 1,2 out of 1,2,3,4)
‚Ä¢ P(Word2=0 | Negative) = 1/3 = 0.33 (opinion 7 out of 5,6,7)
‚Ä¢ P(Word2=0 | Neutral) = 2/3 = 0.67 (opinions 9,10 out of 8,9,10)
P(Positive) √ó P(Word1=0|Pos) √ó P(Word2=0|Pos) = 0.4 √ó 0.25 √ó 0.5 = 0.05
P(Negative) √ó P(Word1=0|Neg) √ó P(Word2=0|Neg) = 0.3 √ó 1.0 √ó 0.33 = 0.1
P(Neutral) √ó P(Word1=0|Neu) √ó P(Word2=0|Neu) = 0.3 √ó 0.67 √ó 0.67 = 0.13
