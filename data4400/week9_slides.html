Data Quality for Forecasting

Essential Data Quality Dimensions

Completeness

  • No missing critical time periods
  • Consistent data collection frequency
  • Appropriate historical depth

Accuracy

  • Correct measurement units
  • Validated data sources
  • Error detection procedures

Consistency

  • Standardized collection methods

  • Minimal reporting delays

Forecasting Model Validation

Validation Approaches

Train-Test Split

Use 70-80% for training, 20-30% for testing

Time Series Cross-Validation

Expanding window validation respecting temporal order

Out-of-Sample Testing

Reserve recent data for final model evaluation

Evaluation Metrics

  • MAE: Mean Absolute Error
  • RMSE: Root Mean Square Error
  • MAPE: Mean Absolute Percentage Error
  • AIC/BIC: Information criteria

Business Metrics

Consider cost of forecast errors in business terms, not just statistical accuracy

Managing Forecast Uncertainty

Sources of Forecasting Uncertainty

Aleatory Uncertainty

  • Inherent randomness in data
  • Natural variation in demand
  • Cannot be reduced with more data

Epistemic Uncertainty

  • Model structure uncertainty
  • Parameter estimation errors
  • Can be reduced with better models/data

Risk Management Strategies

  • Confidence intervals and prediction bands
  • Scenario planning (optimistic/pessimistic)
  • Ensemble forecasting methods
  • Regular model updates and monitoring
  • Robust decision-making frameworks
Key Insight: Perfect forecasts are impossible. Focus on understanding and communicating uncertainty to enable better decision-making.
